# IT Pickup Point: How Smart Lockers Are Transforming Enterprise IT Asset Distribution

The final step in enterprise IT asset distribution has always been the weakest. Procurement is automated. Approval workflows are digital. Asset records live in the ERP. And then — a human IT engineer physically hands a device to an employee, records it on a sign-out sheet, and files it in a shared folder somewhere.

That last step is where audit trails disappear, where scheduling creates delays, and where IT staff time gets consumed by tasks that should not require them.

We solved this by deploying a Signifi Digital Storage Locker as an IT Pickup Point in our office. Here's what changed.

---

## The Problem: Manual Handoff in an Otherwise Automated Process

Our device distribution workflow was well-designed up to a point:

1. Employee raises a device request via SAP Ariba
2. IT Manager approves the request
3. CLEA lifecycle app creates an on-boarding task assigned to IT
4. IT engineer retrieves the device from the storage room
5. **IT engineer schedules a meeting with the employee to hand it over**
6. Both parties sign a handover document
7. IT engineer updates the asset record in SAP ISP

Steps 1–4 and 7 were essentially automated or near-automated. Step 5 was a calendar problem. Step 6 was a paper problem. Step 7 was a follow-up problem.

For 20 devices a month, this was manageable. At scale — new hire waves, office expansions, refresh cycles — it became the bottleneck.

---

## How the End-to-End Workflow Works

**Before (manual):**
> SAP Ariba approval → IT retrieves device → schedules handover meeting → physical sign-out → SAP ISP update

**After (automated with smart locker):**
> SAP Ariba approval → IT loads device into locker compartment → SignifiVISION sends employee notification → employee authenticates at locker → collects device → CLEA/SAP ISP record updated automatically

The IT engineer's role shifts from "appointment maker and device handler" to "stock manager" — they load the locker when orders are ready, and the system handles everything else.

## The Compliance Case: Why Digital Beats Paper

Digital lockers replace the weakest link in asset distribution — the sign-out sheet. An authenticated digital record is the only audit trail that survives a compliance review.

With a smart locker:
- Every access event is tied to an authenticated user (employee ID, badge, PIN)
- Timestamp, compartment number, and asset details are logged automatically
- The record is immutable — stored in SignifiVISION, not in a shared Excel file
- Return events are logged the same way — deposit triggers a ticket, not a memory

For organisations where asset accountability matters — enterprise IT, healthcare, banking, government — this is not a convenience feature. It is the compliance control that manual processes simply cannot provide.

## Three Things That Made the Deployment Successful

**1. Placement drives adoption more than technology.**
We put the locker in the lobby, not the IT room. Visibility and accessibility are the two factors that determine whether employees use a self-service system. A locker in the IT room is still IT-dependent — you need to find someone to let you in.

**2. The notification message is the user experience.**
Employees don't think about the locker until they receive the notification. That message needs to contain exactly what they need: where the locker is, how to authenticate, what to do if something goes wrong. We refined this message twice before collection rates reached target.

**3. Integration removes the last manual step.**
Without integration, someone still has to update the asset record after collection. With CLEA and SAP ISP integration, the collection event triggers the record update automatically. The locker only delivers its full value when it's connected to the asset management system — not running as a standalone appliance.

## Daily Operations: The Delivery Status Lifecycle

Every item managed through the locker system goes through a clear status progression:



**7-day rule:** Items uncollected after 7 days are moved from the locker to the IT Link Center (ITLC) to free compartment space. A new ITLC pickup record is created and the employee is re-notified.

**Emergency key:** A physical emergency key is held by the IT team. It opens all doors in a locker set simultaneously — used when a compartment is stuck or an item was placed in the wrong slot. This does not reset any app records.
