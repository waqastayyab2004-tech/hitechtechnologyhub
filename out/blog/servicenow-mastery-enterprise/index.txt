<h2>Knowledge Base Strategy</h2>
<p>A great KB is the single highest-ROI investment in ServiceNow. Here's the template I used for every KB article:</p>
<pre><code>Title: [Verb] + [System] + [Error/Situation]
Example: "Reset VPN Password After Lockout"

Sections:
1. When to use this article (exact symptoms)
2. Estimated time to resolve
3. Step-by-step (max 7 steps, screenshots)
4. If this doesn't work → escalation path
5. Related articles
</code></pre>
<p><strong>The 80/20 rule of KB:</strong> 20% of your articles will resolve 80% of your tickets. Find those articles first — run a ticket volume report by category for 3 months, identify the top 10 issues, and build excellent KB for those.</p>
<h2>Automation Within ServiceNow</h2>
<p>The three automations that moved our team from reactive to proactive:</p>
<h3>1. Auto-Assignment by Location + Category</h3>
<pre><code class="language-javascript">// ServiceNow Business Rule
if (current.category == 'hardware' &#x26;&#x26; current.location == 'Main Office') {
    current.assignment_group = 'IT Onsite Support - Regional Team';
}
</code></pre>
<p>This alone eliminated 30% of mis-assigned tickets.</p>
<h3>2. SLA Warning Notification at 70%</h3>
<p>Configure a notification that fires when a ticket reaches 70% of its SLA time. Three hours before breach is too late — at 70%, you still have time to act, escalate, or update the customer.</p>
<h3>3. Auto-Resolution for Known Patterns</h3>
<p>If a ticket is resolved and the same user raises the identical issue 3+ times, auto-tag it for Problem Management review. Don't keep treating symptoms — fix root causes.</p>

<h2>The Metric That Actually Matters</h2>
<p>Management always asks about resolution time. The metric I cared about was <strong>First Contact Resolution (FCR)</strong>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>How often do we solve the issue on the first interaction, without the user having to chase us?</p>
</blockquote>
<p>We got FCR above 75% by:</p>
<ol>
<li>Better KB articles (users found answers themselves)</li>
<li>Self-service portal for password resets</li>
<li>Templated responses with next steps always included</li>
<li>Proactive communication — message the user before they chase</li>
</ol>

<p>The engineers who get frustrated with ServiceNow are usually fighting the tool instead of designing their workflows around it.</p>
