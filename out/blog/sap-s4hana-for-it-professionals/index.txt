<h2>SAP HANA: Why It's Different From Every Database You've Seen</h2>
<p>SAP HANA is an <strong>in-memory columnar database</strong>. This means:</p>
<ul>
<li>All data is loaded into RAM, not read from disk at query time</li>
<li>Queries that take minutes in traditional RDBMS take seconds in HANA</li>
<li><strong>RAM is critical</strong> — undersized memory = degraded performance = angry users</li>
</ul>
<h3>What This Means for Infrastructure Teams</h3>
<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Traditional DB</th>
<th>SAP HANA</th>
<th>Your Action</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>I/O bound (disk speed matters most)</td>
<td>Memory bound (RAM is critical)</td>
<td>Right-size memory from day 1</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Horizontal scale common</td>
<td>Scale-up preferred</td>
<td>Plan for large single-server</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Backup to local disk</td>
<td>Backup to HANA Backup API</td>
<td>Integrate with backup tools</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Row-based storage</td>
<td>Column-based compression</td>
<td>Different storage calculations</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>The most common infrastructure mistake with HANA: <strong>RAM swap</strong>. If HANA starts swapping to disk, performance collapses immediately. Monitor RAM utilisation daily, not weekly.</p>
<h2>SAP Basis: The Role Infrastructure Teams Must Understand</h2>
<p>SAP Basis is the administration layer that sits between infrastructure and the business application. Basis administrators handle:</p>
<ul>
<li>System starts, stops, and restarts</li>
<li>Transport management (moving changes between DEV → QA → PRD)</li>
<li>Performance monitoring (SM50, SM66, ST05)</li>
<li>User authorisation (SU01, SU10)</li>
<li>Background job scheduling (SM36, SM37)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What you need to know:</strong> When a Basis team member says "system is slow," they're usually looking at OS-level metrics they can't access directly. Learn their monitoring tools (SAP Solution Manager, CCMS) so you can speak the same language during incident calls.</p>
<h2>SAP BTP: The Cloud Extension Platform</h2>
<p>SAP Business Technology Platform (BTP) is where modern SAP development happens. For IT infrastructure teams supporting BTP:</p>
<h3>Key BTP Services You'll Encounter</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Integration Suite</strong> — connects SAP to non-SAP systems</li>
<li><strong>Extension Suite</strong> — custom apps extending S/4HANA</li>
<li><strong>AI Business Services</strong> — ML APIs for document extraction, text analysis</li>
<li><strong>HANA Cloud</strong> — fully managed HANA database as a service</li>
</ul>
<p>BTP is typically managed by the application team, but infrastructure IT often needs to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Configure network rules for BTP endpoints</li>
<li>Set up corporate proxy exceptions for BTP connectors</li>
<li>Provide SSO integration via Azure AD to BTP subaccounts</li>
</ul>
<h2>SAP Ariba: The Procurement System You Interact With Daily</h2>
<p>If your company uses SAP Ariba for procurement, you as IT are probably:</p>
<ul>
<li>Raising purchase requests (PRs) for IT equipment</li>
<li>Getting approval from IT managers</li>
<li>Creating purchase orders (POs) through Ariba</li>
<li>Receiving equipment against POs</li>
</ul>
<p>Understanding Ariba's PR → PO → GR (Goods Receipt) flow will make your procurement significantly faster. The teams that understand the full workflow never have unnecessary delays.</p>
<h2>SAP Analytics Cloud: Turning Your IT Data Into Decisions</h2>
<p>SAP Analytics Cloud (SAC) is the BI/analytics layer. I used it alongside PowerBI to build IT dashboards for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Monthly SLA performance by category</li>
<li>Asset refresh cycle planning (colour-coded warranty status)</li>
<li>Ticket volume trends by office and engineer</li>
<li>Onboarding completion rates vs. HR targets</li>
</ul>
<p>The key to useful IT analytics: <strong>start with the question your manager asks most often</strong>. Usually it's "what's our SLA % this month?" or "how many assets are due for refresh?" Build the dashboard that answers those questions, and you'll have an audience immediately.</p>
